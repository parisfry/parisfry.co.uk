System Overload

System overload explores the relationship between rave culture, immersive live music experiences, and neurodivergent sensory perception through experimental moving image and print outcomes.

Beginning with audio-reactive visuals created in p5.js, I used rave sound frequencies and rhythms to generate digital patterns inspired by club environments.

These visuals were then translated into physical outcomes through laser engraving onto acrylic, layering, and print-based experimentation.

This project focuses on colour, repetition, and movement to recreate the overwhelming yet immersive atmosphere of club spaces. And in turn explores the intersection between sound, perception, and experimental graphic communication.

Final outcomes include spatially influenced rave posters and flyers, an engraved acrylic book archiving my visual compositions, and a layered CD case exploring alternative approaches to music packaging and design.
