An Ode to the dancefloor

An Ode to the Dancefloor explores rave culture through shared experience, collective movement, and computational design. Dancefloors function as spaces where bodies, rhythm, sound, and light combine to produce patterns of movement and fluidity.

My project uses Creative Computing as a framework to interpret these crowd dynamics. Using both p5.js and Touchdesigner, I aim to simulate, and translate the energy created within these collective experiences.

Through combining algorithmic pattern generation with textile design practices, I create a visual language where movement is abstracted into both digital and physical design. I draw inspiration from the rhythm and flow of people moving together, using code to generate layered patterns, repeated forms, and shifting compositions. These digital outcomes are then developed through print and textile processes, allowing my work to move between screen-based experimentation and physical making.

My project expands textile design beyond static surfaces by bringing patterns into animation, projection, and immersive digital spaces. Through this process, I explore how code, movement, and material can work together to create designs that feel fluid, responsive, and alive.
